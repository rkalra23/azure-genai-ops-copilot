Title: Billing Service Restart Runbook
Version: v3
Effective Date: 2026-01-15
Department: Operations
Doc Type: runbook

## Overview

This document describes the operational procedure for safely restarting the billing service after failures, maintenance windows, or timeout incidents.

## Preconditions

- Confirm no active month-end billing batch is running.
- Verify queue backlog is below 100 messages.
- Notify downstream support teams before restart.

## Restart Procedure

1. Stop the billing service:
   systemctl stop billing-service

2. Verify service shutdown completed successfully.

3. Review recent logs:
   /var/log/billing/billing-service.log

4. Start the billing service:
   systemctl start billing-service

## Validation Steps

1. Validate the health endpoint:
   /health

2. Confirm message consumption resumes within 5 minutes.

3. Verify no backlog growth in Oracle AQ queues.

## Troubleshooting

- If the service does not start, verify Oracle connectivity.
- If requests time out after restart, restart the queue-consumer service.
- If the health check fails for more than 10 minutes, escalate to L2 support.

## Escalation

Escalate to L2 support if:
- restart fails twice
- service remains degraded for more than 15 minutes
- queue backlog exceeds 500 messages
