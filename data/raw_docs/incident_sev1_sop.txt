Title: Severity 1 Incident Handling SOP
Version: v3
Effective Date: 2025-11-10
Department: SRE
Doc Type: sop

## Overview

This SOP defines the operational process for managing Severity 1 (Sev1) incidents involving critical production outages, widespread customer impact, or major platform degradation.

## Severity 1 Criteria

A Severity 1 incident may include:

* complete production outage
* widespread customer impact
* critical billing or payment failures
* platform-wide authentication failures
* major infrastructure instability
* sustained service unavailability

## Initial Response

Within the first 5 minutes:

1. Declare the Severity 1 incident.
2. Create an incident bridge and communication channel.
3. Assign an incident commander.
4. Notify on-call engineers and SRE leadership.
5. Begin impact assessment and service validation.

## Incident Management Procedure

1. Identify affected services and customer impact scope.
2. Assign technical owners for each impacted component.
3. Begin mitigation and containment activities.
4. Provide status updates every 15 minutes.
5. Maintain an active incident timeline and action log.
6. Escalate unresolved blockers immediately.
7. Coordinate rollback or recovery procedures if required.

## Communication Protocol

During the incident:

1. Notify stakeholders and leadership teams.
2. Provide periodic customer impact summaries.
3. Maintain centralized incident notes.
4. Ensure all mitigation decisions are documented.
5. Coordinate external communication through approved channels.

## Resolution & Recovery

After mitigation:

1. Confirm service stability across all impacted systems.
2. Validate customer-facing functionality.
3. Monitor recovery metrics and error rates.
4. Close the incident bridge after stabilization.
5. Archive incident logs and communication history.

## Post-Incident Review

Within 2 business days:

1. Initiate post-incident review (PIR).
2. Document root cause analysis.
3. Identify contributing factors and operational gaps.
4. Define corrective and preventive actions.
5. Track remediation items to completion.

## Escalation

Escalate to senior leadership if:

* customer impact exceeds 30 minutes
* multiple critical systems are affected
* mitigation efforts are unsuccessful
* regulatory or financial impact is suspected
